ALBUMS WE LOVE

Here we share some of our favourite albums.
We all agree that each of these albums hold a curcial position in the world of Metal.
Some are Iconic masterpieces known to the world, while others are hidden gems with a cult following.
However, we all feel that each of these albums innovatively pushed the boundaries of the genre, and inspired countless other artists.

All of these albums are a must listen for any Metalhead.

Master of Puppets (1986)

Metallica

Metallica's Master of Puppets is an absolute masterpiece, a relentless force of musical brilliance. From the thundering drums to the blistering guitar solos, every moment of this album is electrifying. The title track alone is a rollercoaster of adrenaline, with its intricate riffs and haunting lyrics. "Battery" and "Disposable Heroes" showcase the band's raw energy and technical prowess, while "Welcome Home (Sanitarium)" delves into darker, more melodic territory. With its groundbreaking sound and influential impact, "Master of Puppets" remains a timeless classic in the realm of thrash metal. It's an album that never fails to ignite passion and leave listeners in awe.

Reign in Blood (1986)

Slayer

Slayer's Reign in Blood is a relentless onslaught of sheer intensity and ceasless aggression, delivering an unparalleled experience in the realm of thrash metal. Clocking in at just under 30 minutes, it's a whirlwind of ferocious riffs, blistering solos, and thunderous drumming that leaves listeners breathless. Tracks like "Angel of Death" and "Raining Blood" are iconic in their brutality, while "Altar of Sacrifice" and "Postmortem" showcase the band's mastery of atmosphere and dynamics. With its uncompromising sound and unbridled energy, "Reign in Blood" remains a timeless classic that continues to influence and inspire generations of metalheads worldwide.

Scum (1987)

Napalm Death

Napalm Death's Scum is a seismic eruption of unadulterated aggression, setting the blueprint for grindcore's ferocious sonic assault. Clocking in at just over half an hour, it's a relentless barrage of sonic chaos, yet within the cacophony lies a meticulous craftsmanship. Tracks like "You Suffer" and "Multinational Corporations" epitomize the band's raw energy and socio-political fervor. With its lightning-fast tempos, guttural vocals, and razor-sharp guitar riffage, "Scum" remains a defining moment in extreme music history. It's a visceral experience that leaves an indelible mark, showcasing Napalm Death's uncompromising dedication to pushing the boundaries of brutality.

Roots (1996)

Sepultura

Sepultura's Roots is a thunderous declaration of tribal identity and musical innovation, cementing their status as pioneers of nu and groove metal. Released in 1996, it's a potent blend of aggressive riffs, tribal percussion, and socio-political commentary. Tracks like "Roots Bloody Roots" and "Attitude" pulse with primal energy, while "Ratamahatta" infuses indigenous rhythms with blistering metal intensity. The album's collaboration with indigenous Brazilian tribe Xavante adds authenticity and depth to its exploration of cultural roots. With its raw power and revolutionary spirit, "Roots" transcends genre boundaries, inspiring generations of metalheads worldwide and solidifying Sepultura's place in the pantheon of metal legends.
